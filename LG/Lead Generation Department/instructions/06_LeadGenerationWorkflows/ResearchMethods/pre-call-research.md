**Step-by-step Guide: How to Complete the Pre-call Research**

Open Drive, then the Team Lead folder, then the Deep Research folder, or follow the link [https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1HZ64obic0QHSRo1h9Rx4QCzO7hqIVY0l](https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1HZ64obic0QHSRo1h9Rx4QCzO7hqIVY0l)

Click **New** on the left side, select **Google Docs**, then **Create and share**.  
Here, your document is created. In the top left corner, replace the Untitled **document** with the name of the company you will be doing deep research on.

Prepare the document for filling out:  
Create five tabs by clicking the plus (+)  icon on the left side. Name each tab accordingly:

* General Information about the Company  
* Deep Research  
* Summary  
* Lead Info  
* Post Insights

Prepare each tab by copying the template from the Template document.

The form is ready to be filled out.   
As you see, this research is divided into 5 main sections. Follow the instructions carefully to complete each one.

**I. General Information about the Company**

*This section contains general information about the company with which a call is scheduled.* 

Fill in the information for the following fields:

**Company Name** – Copy it exactly from the company’s LinkedIn page.   
**Country** – Take the country from the LinkedIn About tab of the company page, not from the lead’s profile.  
**Industry** – Use the industry tag from LinkedIn.  
**Email / Phone Number** – Use the general contact email and phone listed on the company’s official website.  
**Website / LinkedIn / Facebook / Instagram / Twitter (X)** – Add direct links to the company’s social media and website. Leave blank if any are missing.

**Short Description** – Paste the **Company Info** from the calendar event description you previously prepared.

**II. Deep Research**  
*This section contains all available information about the company gathered using Perplexity.*

Go to Perplexity → Use the Research mode.

Use this prompt:

Do deep research on the company \[Company Name\] from \[Country\]. Link to the company's LinkedIn page: \[link\]. Gather all possible information using the prompt guide.

1\. Basic Company Information  
Goal: Understand who they are, what they do, and their overall level (size, maturity, positioning).

Where to look:  
Official company website  
LinkedIn (company page)  
Crunchbase / PitchBook / Apollo / ZoomInfo  
News in Google News  
Any other available sources

What to look for:  
Company size (number of employees)  
Description (what they do, mission, values)  
Geography (country, office locations)  
Year founded  
Founders / C-level team  
Brands or products

2\. Financial and Investment Information  
Goal: Understand whether they have a budget and if they are growing.

Where to look:  
Crunchbase  
Pitchbook  
Press releases  
Other websites

What to look for:  
Investment rounds (Pre-seed, Seed, Series A, etc.)  
Investors  
Annual revenue (if available)  
Status: startup, scaleup, enterprise

3\. Tech Stack and Processes  
Goal: Understand what technologies they use in order to offer relevant specialists.

Where to look:  
BuiltWith / Wappalyzer (for companies with a web product)  
GitHub (if they have open-source projects)  
Job postings (on their website and on LinkedIn)  
Tech stack on StackShare  
Any other available sources

4\. Decision-Makers and Leadership: Founders, Owners, CTO, CEO, Directors, HR, Heads of Departments  
Goal: to find who can be contacted and who makes decisions.

What to look for:  
Full names and job titles of top-level executives (CEO, CTO, COO, CMO, etc.)  
Heads of departments (Head of HR, Head of Engineering, Head of Marketing, etc.)  
Founders, co-founders, and owners  
Decision-makers involved in hiring, partnerships, or tech/product development  
Internal recruiters or Talent Acquisition leads  
LinkedIn profiles of key contacts  
Verified work emails (via tools like Hunter, Apollo, RocketReach)  
Background info: previous companies, education, experience

5\. Activity and News  
Goal: Identify potential contact triggers (e.g. product launches, team growth).

Where to look:  
LinkedIn posts  
Company blog  
News (Google News)  
Twitter / X  
Any other available sources

What to look for:  
Team growth  
New office openings  
Hiring sprints  
New partnerships  
AI-related initiatives

6\. Needs and Pain Points  
Goal: Understand what challenges they are currently facing.

How to find out:  
Job postings analysis  
Comments and insights on social media  
From news and activity analysis

7\. Competitive Positioning  
8\. Future Outlook

NOTE: 1\. Use Perplexity on your work accounts or your personal Google account. You get three free searches a day.

2\. The country you specify in the prompt should be the country where the company is located, not the lead’s place of residence.

3\. After Perplexity provides you with information about the company, make sure it’s your company. Several companies can have the same name, just like leads can have identical names. Also, AI-generated answers are not always accurate and may contain errors when processing information.

4.Copy the information (Deep Research) prepared by Perplexity and paste it into this section of the document.

**III. Summary**

*This section provides a summary of the company based on the deep research conducted.*

Go to [Claude.ai](http://Claude.ai)

Paste the Deep Research document.

Use this prompt:

“Create a brief summary of the document”

Copy and paste the generated summary in this section of our document.

**IV. Lead Information**

*This section contains information about the lead with whom the call is planned.*

**Information about the lead with whom the call is scheduled.**

Fill in the information for the following fields:  
 **Lead Name:** Add the full name of the person.

**Job title in the company** \[Company Name\]: Add their current job title and the company name.

**Lead's LinkedIn:** Include a direct link to their LinkedIn profile.

**Lead Information:** Go to ChatGPT (LG account) and use this prompt:

“Gather information about the lead  \[Lead\`s Name\], \[Position of the lead\] at the company \[Company Name\]. Link to the lead's LinkedIn profile is \[link\]”

Copy key details about their experience, previous jobs, education, etc.

NOTE: 1.Name the chat **Lead Info \[Your First Name and Last Name\]**.  
2.After ChatGPT provides you with information about  the lead, make sure it’s your company and your lead. Several companies can have the same name, just like leads can have identical names. Also, AI-generated answers are not always accurate and may contain errors when processing information.

**V. LinkedIn Activity Insights**

*This section includes insights about the company generated by ChatGPT based on its recent LinkedIn posts. It may also include suggestions on how the company can improve its social media presence.*

Copy the link from the “Posts” section of the company’s LinkedIn page.

![][image1]

Go to ChatGPT (LG account).

Use this prompt:

“Review the posts on this LinkedIn page \[Company LinkedIn posts link\], provide general insights based on their activity, reveal about the company's focus and strategy.”

Copy the information prepared by ChatGPT and paste it into the appropriate section of the document.

NOTE: Use different chats in ChatGPT for processing lead info and for handling posts. Name the chat **Post Insights  \[Your First Name and Last Name\]**.
