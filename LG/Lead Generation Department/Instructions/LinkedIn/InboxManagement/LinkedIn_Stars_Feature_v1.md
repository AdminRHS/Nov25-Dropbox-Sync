## OVERVIEW

- **Work Efficiently:** Access high-priority leads instantly

## WHAT IS LINKEDIN STARS FEATURE

### Feature Overview

**What It Does:**
LinkedIn Stars allow you to mark specific conversations as important by adding a â­ star icon next to the contact's name in your messaging inbox.

**Where It Appears:**
- LinkedIn inbox (desktop and mobile)
- Conversation list
- Search results within messaging
- Filter options at top of inbox

**Visual Indicators:**
- â­ Yellow star icon next to contact name
- Star appears in both list view and conversation view
- Starred conversations can be filtered separately

---

### How It Works

**Star Status:**
- **Unstarred:** Default state for all conversations
- **Starred:** You manually flagged this conversation
- **Persistent:** Stars remain until you manually remove them
- **Private:** Only you see your stars (not visible to the contact or other team members)

**Important Note:** LinkedIn stars are profile-specific. If multiple team members access the same LinkedIn account, stars are NOT shared between users.

## HOW TO STAR CONVERSATIONS

### Desktop (LinkedIn Website)

**Method 1: From Inbox List**
1. Go to LinkedIn Messaging inbox
2. Hover over the conversation you want to star
3. Click the **â­ star icon** that appears on the right side
4. Star turns yellow = Conversation is now starred

**Method 2: Inside Conversation**
1. Open the conversation with the prospect
2. Look at the top-right corner of the conversation window
3. Click the **â­ star icon** next to the contact's name
4. Star turns yellow = Conversation is now starred

### Daily Workflow with Stars

**Morning Routine (15 min):**
1. Open LinkedIn inbox
2. Click "Starred" filter
3. Review all starred conversations
4. Prioritize 3-5 for immediate follow-up
5. Send follow-up messages
6. Update CRM with any changes

**Throughout the Day:**
- Check starred folder every 2-3 hours
- Respond to replies from starred leads ASAP (within 1 hour)
- Star new interested leads as they appear

**End of Day (10 min):**
1. Review starred conversations
2. Unstar any that are now resolved (called, rejected, etc.)
3. Update CRM statuses
4. Plan tomorrow's follow-ups

---

### Weekly Cleanup

**Every Friday (20 min):**
1. Review ALL starred conversations
2. Categorize each:
   - **Hot:** Still engaged, keep starred
   - **Warm:** Follow up next week, keep starred
   - **Cold:** No response, unstar and update CRM to "No Response"
   - **Converted:** Call scheduled, unstar and track in CRM only

3. Goal: Keep starred folder under 20 conversations
4. Too many stars = loses effectiveness

## COMMON MISTAKES

### Mistake #1: Over-Starring

**Problem:** Starring every conversation "just in case"

**Why It's Bad:**
- Starred folder becomes cluttered
- Defeats purpose of flagging important leads
- Wastes time checking too many conversations

**Solution:**
- Only star if prospect showed CLEAR interest
- Regularly clean up stars weekly
- Use CRM for broader lead tracking

---

### Mistake #2: Forgetting to Update CRM

**Problem:** Star in LinkedIn but forget to update CRM status

**Why It's Bad:**
- Manager can't see lead progress
- Team members duplicate work
- Reporting is inaccurate
- Bonuses not properly tracked

**Solution:**
- ALWAYS update CRM when starring
- Make it a habit: Star â†’ Open CRM â†’ Update status
- Set reminder if you can't do it immediately

---

### Mistake #3: Never Unstarring

**Problem:** Leaving old stars forever, even after lead is cold or converted

**Why It's Bad:**
- Starred folder fills with dead leads
- Hard to find actual hot leads
- Wastes time checking irrelevant conversations

**Solution:**
- Weekly cleanup every Friday
- Unstar anything older than 2 weeks with no reply
- Unstar immediately when call is booked (CRM tracks it now)

---

### Mistake #4: Starring on Wrong Criteria

**Problem:** Starring based on company name/size instead of interest level

**Why It's Bad:**
- Big company with no interest â‰  hot lead
- Small company with urgency > big company with no response
- Stars should reflect engagement, not vanity metrics

**Solution:**
- Star based on THEIR actions (replied, asked questions, expressed need)
- Don't star based on YOUR hopes (big company, famous brand, etc.)
- Reality check: If they're not responding, unstar them

---

### Mistake #5: Ignoring Stars

**Problem:** Starring conversations but never checking the starred folder

**Why It's Bad:**
- Completely defeats the purpose
- Hot leads go cold from delayed responses
- Missed opportunities

**Solution:**
- Check starred folder 3x daily minimum (morning, midday, end of day)
- Set calendar reminders if needed
- Make it part of your daily routine
