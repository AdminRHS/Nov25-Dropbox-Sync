**Steps:**

Create customized templates for each category
Incorporate personalization variables
Draft subject lines that increase open rates
Write compelling body content that provides value

Test templates with small sample group
Gather feedback and refine
Create template library with usage guidelines

**Resources:**
- Current template repository
- Analytics on previous outreach campaign performance
- Email marketing best practices guides
- CRM system for connection history
- A/B testing results from previous campaigns
- Professional copywriting resources

**Instructions:**
Begin by auditing all existing templates used for reconnecting with old connections. Analyze their performance metrics including open rates, response rates, and conversion rates to identify what's working and what isn't. Review the language, tone, length, and structure of current templates. Research current best practices for re-engagement communication, focusing on personalization, value-driven content, and authentic relationship building. Segment your old connections into meaningful categories such as: former clients/customers, past professional colleagues, dormant network connections, alumni, inactive students or program participants, and cold leads who showed initial interest. For each category, create 2-3 template variations that can be personalized based on specific circumstances. Key elements for effective reconnection templates include: personalized opening that references specific shared history or connection point, acknowledgment of time passed without being apologetic, genuine reason for reaching out (not just "checking in"), value proposition or offering (helpful resource, relevant opportunity, interesting information), open-ended question to encourage response, and clear but low-pressure call-to-action. Subject lines should be concise, personal, and intriguing without being clickbait (examples: "Quick question about [specific topic]", "Thought of you when I saw [relevant news]", "[Shared connection] suggested I reach out"). Keep email body concise (150-250 words maximum), focus on the recipient's interests rather than your agenda, and make it easy to respond with a simple yes/no or short answer question. Include personalization variables such as [First Name], [Company], [Last Interaction Date], [Shared Interest/Project], [Mutual Connection] that must be filled in for each message. Create usage guidelines that specify which template to use in which scenario, how much to customize beyond the template, optimal timing for sending, and appropriate follow-up sequences. Test new templates with a small sample of each connection category, measure results, gather any direct feedback, and refine based on data. Organize final templates in an easily accessible library with clear labeling and instructions for use.
