## 2. KEY METHODOLOGY: ASSOCIATIVE LEARNING

The methodology is based on two principles:

### Principle 1: Storytelling
Information is presented as part of a connected, understandable story.

### Principle 2: Anchoring to Existing Knowledge
New abstract concepts (CRM, file system) are connected to everyday phenomena (cooking, cleaning).

## 3. MASCOT & CONTINUOUS STORY

### 3.1. Mascot: Humanoid Goat
**Approved mascot** for administrative documentation.

**Character:** A goat living a human life (cooks, cleans, goes shopping) but retaining goat attributes.

### 3.2. Main Story: Preparing Lasagna
An analogy with food preparation is used to explain workflow sequence.

**Plot:**
A male goat calls the female goat and asks her to prepare lasagna. She has never made it before. This story illustrates workflow stages:

| Stage | Goat's Action | Association |
|-------|---------------|-------------|
| **Daily Log** | Records the call and notes "Lasagna" request | Task initiation |
| **Research** | Doesn't know the recipe, searches in "Grandma's Recipe Book" | Finding solution |
| **Deep Search / Plan** | Analyzes recipe, makes shopping list and action plan | Planning |
| **Tasks** | Cooks step by step | Task execution |

## 8. MEMORY & LEARNING PRINCIPLES

### Why Associations Work:
1. **Story Connection**: Information presented as narrative
2. **Existing Knowledge Anchor**: New concepts linked to familiar experiences
3. **Visual Memory**: Strong imagery aids recall
4. **Emotional Engagement**: Character-driven stories create emotional connection
5. **Repetition Through Story**: Same character across multiple scenarios reinforces learning

### Implementation:
- Every complex concept must have everyday analogy
- Every violation type must have memorable association
- Every tool/system must have physical world equivalent
