## 5. WORKFLOWS & PROCESSES

### Professional Development Through Repetition

**Purpose:** Achieve professionalism by systematizing and repeating workflows hundreds of times

**Steps:**
1. Identify workflow or process to professionalize
2. Document workflow as system/framework
3. Create rules and best practices
4. Repeat workflow systematically
5. Refine based on learnings
6. Achieve automaticity through repetition

**Responsible Parties:** All team members, management

**Tools/Platforms Used:** Documentation tools, call organizer prompts, AI for pattern recognition

**Success Criteria:** Workflows become automatic, require less thought, execute much faster

**Context:** "Professionalism is defined by doing many repetitions of the same task. Professional differs from amateur in that amateur does a little bit of everything once, while professional does the same action or series of actions hundreds of times. Therefore it becomes automatic, requires less thinking, and execution is much faster."

---

### Learn from Mistakes After ONE Project (Not Ten)

**Purpose:** Accelerate improvement by learning from mistakes immediately rather than repeating them across multiple projects

**Steps:**
1. Complete project
2. Immediately conduct post-mortem analysis
3. Document mistakes and learnings
4. Implement fixes in processes/templates/rules
5. Apply corrections to ALL ongoing and future projects (not just the one where mistake occurred)
6. Update team training and documentation

**Responsible Parties:** Project leads, all team members

**Tools/Platforms Used:** Post-mortem templates, documentation systems

**Success Criteria:** Same mistake never repeated across multiple projects

**Context:** "We don't need to make the same mistake 10 times in 10 different projects to finally understand it's a problem. It's enough to do post-mortem after one project and implement the fix into all other projects."

## 6. RULES & BEST PRACTICES

### Use Corporate Resources, Not Personal Tools

**Rule/Practice:** When company has paid subscriptions and corporate tools, employees must use those instead of personal accounts, even if less convenient

**Rationale:** Corporate tools are 10x more powerful (paid vs free), represent investment company made, ensure data stays with company, enable collaboration, professional appearance

**Application:** All work should use corporate AI subscriptions (Gemini, etc.), not personal free accounts

**Examples:** StoryBoard team needs to switch from personal Gemini to corporate paid Gemini for 10x capacity increase; "Question is not what's comfortable for you or how you work, question is how we as company can provide better results"

---

### Professionalism = Repetition (Professional vs Amateur)

**Rule/Practice:** Professionalism is achieved by doing same task/workflow hundreds of times until automatic

**Rationale:** Professionals differ from amateurs by volume of repetition; amateur does everything a little bit, professional repeats same actions hundreds of times until automaticity, speed increases dramatically

**Application:** Create systems and frameworks, then repeat them systematically; document workflows so they can be repeated

**Examples:** "Professional from amateur differs exactly in that amateur does a little bit of everything, one time each, while professional does same action or same series of actions hundreds of times. Therefore it's brought to automaticity, doesn't require as much thinking, and execution is much faster."

---

### Mid-Development Reviews Prevent Rework

**Rule/Practice:** Show work in progress (not just completed work) to get feedback and make corrections mid-stream

**Rationale:** Prevents building entire system wrong direction; allows course correction; identifies integration needs early; reduces wasted effort

**Application:** On any project lasting weeks/months, schedule reviews at 30%, 50%, 70% completion, not just at end

**Examples:** Dashboard development - review mid-month (~15th) and end of month (~2-3rd), not just when "finished"

---

### Dashboards Must Answer Questions, Not Just Show Data

**Rule/Practice:** When designing dashboards, define specific questions the dashboard should answer; don't just visualize data for sake of visualization

**Rationale:** "Looking at data with eyes is nice, looks great, numbers are fine" but purpose is insight not decoration; must drive decisions

**Application:** For each dashboard card/visualization, ask "what decision does this enable?" or "what question does this answer?"

**Examples:** Call activity dashboard - don't just show numbers, enable analysis like "would this card design look better visually?" to drive UI improvements

---

### Fix the Formula, Not Individual Instances

**Rule/Practice:** When fixing problems, fix the underlying formula/template/system, not each individual occurrence

**Rationale:** Fixing template once improves all future instances; fixing individuals is endless and doesn't prevent future occurrences

**Application:** Message templates, email responses, any repeated communication or process

**Examples:** Instead of correcting each lead generation message individually, create component-based formula vocabulary so all future messages are structured correctly

---

### Friday Rotation: Weak Link Exits, New Player Enters

**Rule/Practice:** Implement weekly Friday evaluation where weakest performing employee exits and new hire enters ("reality show" style rotation)

**Rationale:** "I cannot endlessly hire up people" - quality control through competitive pressure; ensures only strong performers remain; creates urgency for improvement

**Application:** Employee evaluation system with dashboard showing all performance metrics; Friday becomes evaluation day; systematic rotation

**Examples:** "Like reality show, like Dom-2, with cameras. Friday comes, everyone gathers, we watch 'Weak Link', weak one falls out, new one enters."

---

### $300 + Full-Time + Foreign Language = Immediate Interview

**Rule/Practice:** Candidates wanting $300 salary, willing to work full-time, with any foreign language skills get fast-tracked to immediate interview (within days)

**Rationale:** These are "gold nuggets" in the pan - rare and valuable; competitors will hire them quickly; cannot afford to leave them waiting for weeks

**Application:** HR screening and interview scheduling; Tuesday/Thursday interview slots reserved for these candidates

**Examples:** "When you go panning for gold in Alaska and a nugget appears that shines, you immediately grab it"; "Even poor English, not ideal, or other foreign language like German, that's our gold"

---

### Do NOT Travel to Belarus If Heading to Odessa

**Rule/Practice:** Analyze work in progress to ensure heading in right direction; don't wait until destination to realize you went wrong way

**Rationale:** No need to complete entire journey to wrong destination to know you should turn around

**Application:** Regular progress analysis, stakeholder check-ins, milestone reviews

**Examples:** "You're driving somewhere approximately to Odessa but realize you're heading to Belarus - you don't have to drive all the way to Belarus to understand you went the wrong way"
